Carribean Novels

NAD is a web application designed to facilitate the comparative study of literary novels written by novelists of African descent throughout the Diaspora. Built around a database and developed with client-side design tools, the application catalogs the metadata of over 300+ novels and offers general readers and scholars search and data visualization tools to collect data on the literary works. Users will be able to collect data by querying the catalog's database and formulating results through range-finders, various charts and graphs, and maps. From the catalog data and the data sets created with the tools, users will gain knowledge about the various novels that exist and will be able to examine the interconnectedness of these works based on the bibliographic and subject content. It will allow users to formulate research questions to collectively examine and understand the development and evolution of novels written by those of African descent throughout the world.

Afro-Brazilian Novels

The resources used to build the NAD catalog include the bio-bliographical sourcebooks African American Authors, 1745-1945 and Contemporary African American Novelists edited by Emmanuel S. Nelson; the Ringshout website; the African Studies Center in Leiden website; and my very own bookshelves full of novels from novelist of African dewcent whose works I have taught over the years. One crucial part for the growth of NAD is the crowdsourcing for metadata contained in the catalog. There are thousands of literary novels that have been written, so attempting to catalog all of them is a serious feat. The actual entry for each novel is crowdsourced from scholars, students, and librarians through the ??? list- serve and from personal teaching colleagues from various literary department sround the world.
